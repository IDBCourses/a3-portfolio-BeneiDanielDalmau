Arduino Creature

An Interactive Robotic Companion

This project centers on creating a small autonomous creature using Arduino as its brain. The creature uses a combination of sensors—including ultrasonic distance sensors, light sensors, and a tilt sensor—to perceive its surroundings and respond accordingly. Based on environmental inputs, it exhibits behaviors like moving toward light sources, retreating from obstacles, and emitting sounds or LED patterns that reflect its "mood." The physical build incorporates servo motors for movement, giving the creature the ability to scuttle, turn, or adjust its posture. The body was constructed from lightweight materials including cardboard and 3D-printed parts, keeping the design accessible and modifiable. The creature's personality emerges from the programmed logic: it becomes "curious" when detecting movement, "calm" in stable conditions, and "startled" by sudden changes. This project served as an exploration of physical computing fundamentals, including circuit design, sensor integration, and behavioral programming. It demonstrates how simple components can combine to create surprisingly lifelike interactions, bridging the gap between digital code and physical expression. The modular nature of the design allows for future iterations with additional sensors or more complex behaviors.
