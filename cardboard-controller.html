Cardboard Controller

Materiality & Form: Modeling a console Controller

This project serves as a deep dive into form-finding and the structural potential of corrugated cardboard. The objective was to replicate the iconic, highly ergonomic geometry of a gaming console controller—a shape defined by its seamless blends and complex compound curves—using only flat, rigid sheets of material. The process involved breaking down the controller's anatomy into a series of structural ribs and skins. To achieve the signature "horns" and curved grips, I employed radial scoring to create precise, parallel incisions that allow the cardboard to bend smoothly without buckling. Internal structural layers formed a skeleton that maintains shell integrity while handling the pressure of a human grip, while careful attention to the paper's natural grain enabled organic transitions between the buttons and chassis. Beyond the aesthetic challenge, the project explored cardboard as a prototyping medium for product design. It demonstrates that before moving to 3D printing or clay modeling, cardboard provides a tactile, immediate way to test scale, button placement, and hand feel. This study highlights the intersection of manual craftsmanship and industrial design, proving that complex ergonomics can be effectively analyzed through accessible, hands-on material exploration.
